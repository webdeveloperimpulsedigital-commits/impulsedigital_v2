What to Know Before You Go Always-On

What is Always-On Intelligence?

+

Always-On Intelligence is a continuous monitoring programme that tracks brand health, consumer conversations, marketplace sentiment, competitor activity, and reputation risks in real time.

How is this different from social listening?

+

Social listening is one part of it. Always-On Intelligence connects social conversations with reviews, e-commerce signals, news, competitor movement, dashboards, alerts, and strategic interpretation.

What can we track?

+

You can track brand mentions, product conversations, competitor activity, campaign response, sentiment shifts, reviews, ratings, complaint themes, category conversations, and high-priority risk topics.

What do we receive?

+

Depending on the engagement, you can receive live dashboards, custom alerts, weekly reports, monthly deep dives, crisis watch notes, and leadership-ready summaries.

How do alerts work?

+

Alerts are designed around priority signals. These may include negative sentiment spikes, sudden volume increases, recurring complaints, competitor moves, campaign issues, or high-risk mentions.

Is this only for crisis management?

+

No. Crisis tracking is one use case. Always-On Intelligence also helps with brand health, campaign learning, e-commerce improvement, competitor movement, consumer sentiment, and opportunity spotting.

Who should use this inside the organisation?

+

Brand teams, digital teams, corporate communications, e-commerce teams, consumer insights teams, leadership teams, and any function responsible for reputation, customer perception, or market response.

Is this a one-time project?

+

No. This works best as an ongoing intelligence programme. The value comes from continuous visibility, timely alerts, and regular interpretation of what is changing.

When should we start?

+

Start before a major campaign, product launch, category shift, crisis-sensitive period, or any moment where being late to the signal could cost the brand.
