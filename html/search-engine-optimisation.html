What to Know Before You Optimise for Search

What does SEO include?

+

SEO includes the work needed to improve how your brand is found, understood, and trusted through search. This can include technical SEO, content strategy, on-page optimisation, local SEO, ecommerce SEO, B2B SEO, enterprise SEO, authority building, and AI search readiness.

How is SEO different from just writing blogs?

+

Blogs are one part of SEO. A serious SEO system connects search intent, website structure, technical health, authority, page experience, and conversion paths.

How do you decide what SEO work matters first?

+

We begin by finding the biggest constraint. Sometimes it is technical. Sometimes it is content. Sometimes it is poor page structure. Sometimes the brand has authority offline, but weak proof online.

How long does SEO take to work?

+

SEO usually compounds over time. Directional movement can begin within three to six months, but timelines depend on competition, website health, content depth, authority, and how quickly improvements are implemented.

Do you guarantee first-page rankings?

+

No.
Search does not work that way.
Any partner guaranteeing rankings without understanding your category, competition, website, keywords, and implementation control is selling certainty they do not own.

What should SEO be measured by?

+

Rankings matter, but they are not enough. We also look at qualified traffic, enquiry quality, engagement, local actions, conversion movement, product visibility, content performance, and what search is teaching the business.

Can you handle local SEO?

+

Yes. Local SEO helps businesses improve visibility across Google Business Profile, maps, local pages, citations, reviews, and location-led search behaviour.

Can you handle ecommerce SEO?

+

Yes. eCommerce SEO focuses on product visibility, category structure, metadata, internal linking, schema, search-led merchandising, and conversion paths.

Can you handle B2B and enterprise SEO?

+

Yes. B2B and enterprise SEO need more than rankings. They need authority, stakeholder intent mapping, technical governance, scalable content systems, and reporting that supports long buying cycles.

Does AI change SEO?

+

Yes. Search is becoming more answer-led, conversational, and context-heavy. Brands need clearer entities, stronger proof, better structure, and content that helps both people and AI systems understand what they do.
